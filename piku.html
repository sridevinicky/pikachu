Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why would anyone want to clap to scroll?
A: Great question! Perfect for when your hands are dirty, you're eating, or you just want to feel like a wizard controlling the internet with sound.
Q: How does it detect claps vs other sounds?
A: We analyze the audio frequency spectrum and look for sharp, sudden volume spikes characteristic of hand claps. The algorithm filters out background noise and other sounds.
Q: What if I'm in a quiet library?
A: The sensitivity can be adjusted! Quiet finger snaps work too, or you can use the fallback test buttons for silent browsing.
Q: Does it work with different clap styles?
A: Yes! The algorithm adapts to different clapping styles, from golf claps to thunderous applause. Cupped hands work best for detection.
Q: Is this actually useful?
A: Surprisingly yes! Great for accessibility, hands-free browsing while cooking, or when your mouse/trackpad isn't working. Plus it's just fun!
